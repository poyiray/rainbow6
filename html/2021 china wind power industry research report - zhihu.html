china wind power industry market overview - wind energy, wind power concept and resource zone division

definition of wind energy

wind energy refers to the kinetic energy generated by the movement of air, which is a form of transformation of solar energy. due to the uneven heating of various parts of the earth's surface caused by solar radiation, the pressure distribution in the atmosphere is unbalanced, and under the action of the horizontal pressure gradient, the air moves in a horizontal direction to form a wind. wind energy is a renewable clean energy source with large reserves and wide distribution, but its energy density is low and unstable. under certain technical conditions, wind energy can be developed and utilized as an important energy source. wind energy utilization is a comprehensive engineering technology that converts the kinetic energy of the wind into mechanical, electrical and thermal energy through the wind turbine.

definition of wind power

that is, wind power generation, belongs to renewable energy, clean energy, wind power generation refers to the kinetic energy of the wind into mechanical kinetic energy, and then the mechanical energy into electric kinetic energy.

china's wind energy resources are divided into zones

from the perspective of regional distribution of wind power in china, onshore wind resources are mainly concentrated in the "three norths" region, offshore wind resources are concentrated in the southeast coast, and the sea wind speed is significantly greater than that of land. the southeast coast and its islands are the largest wind energy resource areas in china; inner mongolia and northern gansu are the sub-largest wind energy resource areas in china; the eastern part of heilongjiang and jilin and the coastal areas of the liaodong peninsula are also relatively abundant; the northern and coastal areas of the qinghai-tibet plateau and the three northern regions are also of high quality; yunnan-guichuan, gansu, southern shaanxi, henan, western hunan, the mountainous areas of fujian, guangdong and guangxi, and the tarim basin are the smallest wind energy areas in china.

china wind power industry market overview - wind power classification: offshore wind power and onshore wind power

classification of forms of wind power generation

wind power in china can be divided into offshore wind power generation and onshore wind power according to its geographical location. wind farms are divided into two categories: onshore wind farms and offshore wind farms. offshore wind farms include tidal and subtidal tidal flats wind farms, offshore wind farms and deep-sea wind farms.

onshore wind farms: refers to wind farms developed and constructed in tidal flats above the annual average tidal high tide line on land and coast, including wind farms developed and constructed on islands with fixed residents.

tidal and subtidal tidal flats wind farms: refers to wind farms developed and constructed in the sea areas below the multi-year average tidal high tide line along the coast to a water depth of 5 m below the theoretical minimum tide level.

offshore wind farms: refers to wind farms developed and constructed in sea areas with a water depth of 5 m to 50 m below the theoretical minimum tide level, including wind farms developed and constructed on islands and reefs without fixed residents in the corresponding development sea areas.

deep-sea wind farms: refers to wind farms developed and constructed in sea areas 50m below the theoretical minimum tide level, including wind farms developed and constructed on islands and reefs without fixed residents in the corresponding development areas.

offshore wind power has the following advantages

high wind speed, low wind shear: because the sea surface is very smooth, the roughness is small, and the friction is small. therefore, the wind speed is larger, the change in wind speed and wind direction is smaller, and the wind shear (that is, the change of wind speed with height) is also small, so that there is no need for a high tower, which can reduce the cost of wind turbines.

low turbulence: the turbulence of the offshore wind has small turbulence intensity, has a stable dominant wind direction, the unit bears a low fatigue load, and the fan life is longer.

High output: Offshore wind farms allow wind turbines with a larger capacity of a single unit, up to 5MW to 10MW, due to the low noise requirements, through higher rotational speed and voltage, higher energy output can be obtained. At present, China's offshore developable wind energy resources are 3 times that of onshore wind energy resources.

the technology and core device of china's wind power industry - the operation principle of wind power generation

the principle of wind power generation: use wind power to drive the windmill blade to rotate, and then increase the speed of rotation through the speed increaser to promote the generator to generate electricity. according to windmill technology, the speed of about three meters per second (the degree of breeze) can start to generate electricity. wind power does not require the use of fuel and does not produce radiation or air pollution. the device required for wind power generation is called a wind turbine. this kind of wind turbine can be roughly divided into three parts: wind turbine, generator and tower.

technology and core device of china's wind power industry - wind turbine technology path analysis

at present, the technical routes taken by the world's mainstream onshore and offshore wind power manufacturers mainly focus on doubly-fed asynchronous, permanent magnet direct drive and permanent magnet semi-direct drive these three technical routes. doubly-fed fans usually have a small single-unit capacity and flexible installation, but using a gearbox and a wind turbine link, the gearbox is a component that is easy to overload and has a relatively high damage rate, while the double-fed gearbox speed is usually relatively high, so the maintenance cost is also higher. direct drive fan is currently a form widely adopted by foreign wind turbine manufacturers, because there is no gearbox, so the maintenance cost is relatively low, but the direct drive fan is usually larger in the same capacity, and the lifting cost is higher.

the technical route of permanent magnet semi-direct drive synchronous wind turbines is more in line with the current development of offshore wind power in china. at present, china's machine manufacturers of bearings, high-speed gearbox and other core components are still largely dependent on foreign imports, permanent magnet semi-direct drive synchronous wind turbine combines the advantages of double feed and permanent magnet direct drive two technical routes, the use of low and medium speed gearbox transmission, the bearing, gearbox manufacturing process requirements are relatively low, the overall structure of the unit is more compact, conducive to transportation and hoisting, more suitable for the development of china's offshore wind power at this stage.

technology and core device of china's wind power industry - wind turbine: core wind turbine

the devices required for wind power generation are called wind turbines. this kind of wind turbine is mainly composed of wind wheel blades, gearboxes, generators, bearings, hubs and control systems and towers. in general, it can be divided into three parts: wind turbine, generator and tower. among them, the localization process of major components has been basically completed, and the replacement rate has reached more than 96%.

wind turbines can be divided into asynchronous and synchronous wind turbines according to the structure and working principle of the generator, asynchronous fans can be divided into cage-type asynchronous fans and winding doubly-fed asynchronous fans according to their rotor winding structure, and synchronous fans can be divided into permanent magnet synchronous fans and electric excitation synchronous fans according to their rotor excitation methods.

the composition of offshore wind turbines, the technology and core device of china's wind power industry

offshore wind turbines are slightly different from onshore wind turbines in structure, and their structure can be divided into tower heads (wind turbines and engine rooms), towers and foundations (underwater structures and foundations). its structure is diverse, and can be divided into single pile foundation, multi-pile foundation such as ordinary multi-pile foundation, three-legged pile foundation, gravity foundation, suction foundation, suspension foundation and so on.

LEVELIZED KWH COST (LCOE)

LCOE (Levelized Cost of Electricity) is an internationally common indicator for evaluating the cost of electricity, which can measure the economy of power generation from the entire project cycle of wind power. LCOE is determined by early initial investment costs, operating costs, maintenance costs, failure costs, other potential costs and salvage value gains.

china's wind power industry policies and regulations - electricity prices and subsidy policies (part 1 of 2)

looking back at the history of wind power development, the wind power industry has gradually promoted the localization of wind turbines from the support of subsidies, the scale and variety of wind turbines have continued to increase, and the endogenous growth capacity of the industry has steadily improved, gradually moving towards the era of parity. in may 2018, centralized wind power projects officially entered the "bidding on the internet" stage from the "benchmark electricity price" stage. in may 2019, the national development and reform commission (ndrc) began to guide electricity prices, adjusting the electricity prices of onshore and offshore wind power projects from benchmark feed-in tariffs to guidance prices as the upper limit for enterprises to declare feed-in tariffs. in 2021, onshore wind power will withdraw subsidies, and the wind power industry will enter the era of parity.

according to statistics, before the end of 2020, the land wind parity base has been approved, and the short-term subsidy has been driven by the decline of the slope to rush the installation, and the long-term industry development trend has not changed. in may 2019, the national development and reform commission issued the notice on improving the wind power feed-in tariff policy, proposing that onshore wind power will no longer be subsidized after 2021. in 2020, the state issued a number of policies such as the several opinions on promoting the healthy development of non-hydro renewable energy power generation and the notice on announcing the grid parity projects for wind power and photovoltaic power generation in 2020, which orderly promote the de-subsidization process of the wind power industry, ensure the scientific management of wind power consumption and wind power project construction, and continue to promote the transformation of the energy structure。

in january 2020, the ministry of finance, the national development and reform commission, and the national energy administration issued the several opinions on promoting the healthy development of non-hydro renewable energy power generation: new offshore wind power projects will no longer be subsidized in 2022. the central government encourages local governments to subsidize themselves to support the construction of offshore wind power projects in the province; at the same time, it is proposed that "renewable energy power generation projects included in the subsidy catalogue shall approve the amount of central financial subsidies according to the number of reasonable utilization hours".

introduction of key enterprises in the global wind power industry - vestas (1/2)

Vestas is a global wind power leader integrating the design, manufacture, sales, installation and service of wind turbines. Founded in 1945, Vestas Wind Systems A/S spun off in 1986 and merged with Danish wind turbine manufacturer NEG in 2004 to expand its market share to 32%. Headquartered in Denmark, the company's business includes two segments, onshore wind turbine sales and service, and the offshore business is jointly operated with Mitsubishi. The business area covers the Americas, Europe, the Middle East, Africa and Asia Pacific.

company history

In 1979, the company began a business transformation to produce wind turbines. In 2003, the company merged with Danish wind turbine manufacturer NEGMicon to create VestasWindSystemsA/S, the world's largest manufacturer of wind turbines. The company set competitive wind power targets in 2005, opened its first Chinese production facility in Tianjin in 2006, and announced on December 1, 2008 that it would expand its North American headquarters in Portland, Oregon, USA. In 2009, Vestas began to promote the introduction of new wind turbines into the market, through a number of acquisitions and co-creation of wind turbine companies, gradually becoming a global wind turbine industry leader.

company operating conditions

In 2020, Vestas achieved revenues of 14,819 million euros, of which 86% came from the power plant solutions business (mainly onshore wind turbine sales, EPCs, etc.). In 2020, Vestas has 13 production bases, including 6 assembly plants, 5 blade plants, 1 control plant, 1 wind tower plant, Vestas' service business mainly includes AOM2000-5000, in the past few years, the service varieties have become more and more concentrated, AOM4000 or more service scope accounted for more than 90% of the total service installed capacity.
