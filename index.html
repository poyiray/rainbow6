2.2.1 three-circle circulation and air pressure belt

global regular atmospheric motion, often referred to as atmospheric circulation. atmospheric circulation transports heat and water vapor from one region to another, thus exchanging heat and water vapor between high and low latitudes, between oceans and land. since there are laws to follow in atmospheric circulation, mastering the laws can respond to changes with invariance.



because it is necessary to take the textbook as the core, when understanding, students are taken to explain the causes of the three circles of circulation and the air pressure belt from the text and images on the textbook.

first, a single circle circulation



according to the principle of thermal circulation, the most fundamental cause of atmospheric movement is the uneven heating and cold of the ground. globally, equatorial regions have low latitudes and higher ground temperatures; in this way, a thermal circulation is formed between the equator and the poles due to the uneven heat and cold. this ideal atmospheric motion is called a single-circle circulation.

there are two hypothetical conditions for the formation of a single-circle circulation: (1) uniformity of the earth's surface

(2) the earth does not rotate, and there is no geostatic deflection force

next, on the basis of hypothetical condition (1), the earth's rotational deflection force exists, and a three-circle circulation is formed.



  1. three-circle circulation with air pressure with wind belt
  2. causes and distribution of air pressure belt wind bands

for this knowledge point, the most critical point is that comprehension memory, on the basis of independently drawing an image, will analyze the causes of each air pressure belt and wind belt.

at the equator, temperatures are higher due to the high amount of solar radiation. updrafts prevail when the atmosphere is heated, while a belt of low pressure is formed in the equatorial region. because in the equatorial region, it is named "equatorial low pressure zone".


vertical movement of the atmosphere will cause differences in air pressure on the horizontal plane, and the updraft in the equatorial region will be deflected to high latitudes after being subjected to horizontal pressure gradient forces and rotational deflection forces. the atmosphere begins to sink under the influence of gravity due to a large amount of accumulation just above 30°, and a high pressure belt is formed near the ground due to the sinking air flow near 30°. this location is close to the tropics but not tropical, and is named the "subtropical high pressure belt", one in each of the northern and southern hemispheres.

the subtropical high pressure zone of the sinking air current is blocked by the ground and radiates around, two of which move to the north and south sides. as shown in the figure, after the air flow returns to the equator, a circulation is formed at low latitudes, called a low-latitude circulation. the other stream of air travels towards high latitudes.


near 90 °, the solar radiation is weak, the temperature is the lowest, the atmosphere is contracted by the cold and sinks, forming a high-pressure center near the ground, named "extremely low and high pressure belt".

the sinking of cold air in the polar high pressure zone is blocked by the ground and radiates in all directions (lower latitudes), and these cold air meets warm air from the subtropical high pressure zone near 60° to form a polar front. (the encounter between cold and warm air forms a face called a front.) the front near 60° is close to the polar region, so it is called "polar front". warm air is light, cold air is heavy, warm air is lifted by cold air to passively form updraft, forming a low pressure near the ground, named "subpolar low pressure belt". at the same time, the middle and high latitude circulations are also formed, and they are called three-circle circulation together with the low-latitude circulation.

near the ground, 7 air pressure bands are formed accordingly. the air pressure belt distribution is characterized by:

(1) the distribution of high pressure belt and low air pressure belt;

(2) the genesis of the equatorial low pressure belt and the polar high pressure belt belongs to the thermal cause, and the cause of the subtropical high pressure belt and the subpolar low pressure belt belongs to the dynamic cause. thermal causes refer to the movement of the atmosphere for hot and cold reasons; dynamic reasons refer to the movement of the atmosphere for non-thermal reasons.



the appearance of a difference in air pressure near the ground causes the atmosphere to move horizontally, forming a wind band between the barometric pressure bands. draw these winds first according to the way you paint them. the names of the wind belts are:

2. the nature of the air belt of the air pressure belt (influence on climate)

the impact on climate is analyzed from the two aspects of temperature and precipitation, and the temperature is described as "high temperature, mild and cold", and precipitation is described as "rainy, wet and dry".

for example: (generally, when precipitation is small, the temperature is described as "hot", and precipitation is more described as "high temperature".) ）

in this way, the properties of each air pressure belt and wind belt are analyzed:

equatorial low pressure zone: high temperature and rainy

subtropical high-pressure zone: hot and dry

subpolar low pressure zone: cold and wet

polar high pressure zone: cold and dry

trade wind belt: hot and dry

zephyr belt: mild and humid

east wind belt: cold and dry

3. seasonal movement of the air pressure belt wind belt - "point north belt north, point south belt south"

as the sun's direct point moves north-south, the pressure belt wind belt also moves north and south. however, the movement of the air pressure belt wind belt has a certain lag, and it is not moved at the same time as the direct point of the sun. in this way, a place on earth will not be controlled by only one air pressure belt or wind belt all year round, but may be controlled by two different air pressure belts or wind belts alternately. the air pressure belt wind has different properties, so the areas subject to alternating control will also show different climatic characteristics throughout the year.

finally, don't forget that the distribution of the global air pressure belt wind belt is premised on the uniform nature of the lower cushion surface, and the impact of the sea and land distribution on the atmospheric circulation is not considered for the time being.
